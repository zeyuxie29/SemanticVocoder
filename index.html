SemanticVocoder

Bridging Audio Generation and Audio Understanding via Semantic Latents

| Paper | Code |

Zeyu Xie 1,2, Chenxing Li 2, Mengyue Wu 3, Yuexian Zou 1

1 ADSP Lab, Peking University 2 Tencent 3 X-LANCE Lab, Shanghai Jiao Tong University

zeyuxie25@stu.pku.edu.cn

Abstract.

Existing audio generation models typically rely on VAEs (Variational Autoencoders) and perform generation within the VAE latent space. Although VAEs excel at compression and reconstruction, their latents naturally encapsulate low-level acoustic details rather than semantically discriminative information, leading to entangled event semantics and complicating the training of generative models. To address these issues, we discard VAE acoustic latents and introduce semantic encoder latents, and thus propose SemanticVocoder, a generative vocoder which directly synthesizes waveforms from semantic latents. Equipped with SemanticVocoder, our text-to-audio generation model achieves a lowest Fréchet Distance and Fréchet Audio Distance scores on the AudioCaps test set, as the introduced semantic latents exhibit better discriminability than the acoustic VAE latents. While achieving better generation performance, it also serves as a promising trial toward unifying understanding and generation within shared semantic space.

SemanticVocoder Construction


Pipeline

An overview of SemanticVocoder training, downstream TTA training, and downstream tasks inference. Blue arrow The SemanticVocoder training: the input audio is fed into a semantic encoder to extract semantic latents, which serve as conditions to train the flow-matching network for waveform prediction. Red arrow The audio generative DiT training: the input text is processed by a text encoder to obtain textual features, which are used to train the DiT model for generating semantic latents. Black arrow Downstream task inference: Equipped with SemanticVocoder, both audio generation and understanding tasks can be performed within the same semantic latent space.
